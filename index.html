During the summer of 2014, I interned at VMware on the Project Nee team. Project Nee is a hands on computer learning service. By combining an in-brower virtual machine with manuals, employees can receive hands-on training anywhere. However, many of the trainers who want to set up a virtual machine do not have technical backgrounds. Therefore, I was tasked with designing a better way for non-technical users to set up virtual machines and applications. VMware also requires all interns to create a poster documenting their intern project, and I won 2nd place in the Cambridge office poster contest.

Links Full Infographic
For a research project, I wanted to analyze harassment and gender roles in video games. The current video game environment is extremely toxic towards girls, as females are constantly harassed online. While the objectification and sexualization of female characters contribute to the toxicity in video games, the idea of masculinity and gender roles for men also breed an environment for harassment. This infographic is meant to demonstrate the idea that in order to progress the field towards a more equal environment, we must break gender roles for females and males.

Designed the packaging
For this branding project, I designed the logo, custom patterned wrapping paper, and gift box for a soap kit. The gift item was to be based on a critter in the park; I chose the bee. Bees release a pheromone to call to other bees when they are stressed. In order to reflect this behavior, I chose to create a soap kit, which relaxes one through scents (which are made with bee products.
